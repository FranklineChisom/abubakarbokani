The Twelfth Amendment and the ERA

92 U. Chi. L. Rev. (forthcoming 2026) [SSRN]

Whether the Equal Rights Amendment is—right now—part of the Constitution is a matter of serious debate. But one question has received less attention: whether the ERA was ever proposed in the first place. Under the Twelfth Amendment's procedures for electing the President, a valid proposal requires "a majority of the whole number" of each house of Congress. If the same rule applies to constitutional amendments, then the ERA might have fallen short in the 92nd Congress, which had five vacancies when the amendment was approved.
